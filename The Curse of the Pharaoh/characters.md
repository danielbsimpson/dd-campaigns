## Act 0 — Woodvost

### The Three Merchants

Three foreign-looking travelers who approach the party before the campaign begins. They are citizens of Saqaar, come to seek heroes who can save their city from the curse of Pharaoh Seti.

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Amunet | Foreign merchant / emissary | One of three travelers; her colorful silken robes and exotic accent mark her as far from home. She and her companions are desperate for help. |
| Kasir | Foreign merchant / emissary | Travels alongside Amunet and Nadir; leads the camels and mules laden with exotic goods. |
| Nadir | Foreign merchant / emissary | The one who steps forward to speak; thick accent, cautious smile. Presents the party with their mission — save the great city of Saqaar. |

## Act 1 — City of Saqaar

### Royal Council

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| The City Council | Ruling body; main quest-giver | A collective governing voice that speaks as one from a balcony above the council hall, though different members speak each time. They inform the party of the unnatural sandstorms, the rumors of Pharaoh Seti's return, and officially commission the adventurers to find the Ankh of Eternity. They offer gold and residency in Saqaar as reward. |

---

### Temple of Ra

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Imam Zakariya al-Masri | Priest of Ra / spiritual advisor | A wise and elderly man with a calm demeanor and a deep, resonant voice. Deeply respected for his knowledge of ancient texts. In Act 1 he can offer spiritual guidance, historical context, and blessings. In Act 4, he identifies the pieces of Ra's Sundial, explains the vision of Anubis's return, and directs the party toward the Androsphinx's Oasis to find the final piece. |

---

### The Market

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Fatima bint Rashid | Market stall merchant | Runs one of the busiest stalls in the market, selling exotic spices, rare artifacts, and a wide variety of goods. Sharp, charismatic, and well-connected — she has her ear to the ground and can share city gossip and current events. A formidable negotiator, but fair to those who show respect. |
| Nazir ibn Yusuf | Travelling merchant / Lost Caravan quest-giver | A jovial, warm, and well-traveled merchant who is first encountered at the city gates in distress over his missing caravan. Once his goods are recovered (see *The Lost Caravan*), he sets up his stall in the market. He is generous, loves to share stories of his travels, and offers discounts and trinkets to loyal customers. Rewards the party with a Ring of Fire Elemental Command. |

---

### The Grand Entrance

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| The Frantic Merchant | Minor NPC / introduction hook | An unnamed distressed citizen who rushes up to the party as they are escorted toward Saqaar's entrance. He has heard about the city council's call to arms and desperately pleads for help, hinting at the chaos the curse has caused even among ordinary people. |

---

### The Shisha Lounge

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Hassan al-Karim | Shisha Lounge owner / local informant | Friendly and outgoing owner of the Shisha Lounge, where locals and travelers come to unwind. A master storyteller with extensive knowledge of the city's history and current events. His connections with both common folk and the elite make him a key source of rumors and intelligence. Can point the party toward the Scarab Cult and the missing explorers. |

---

### The Turkish House

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Yusuf ibn Farid | Tavern proprietor / former adventurer | A jovial man with a hearty laugh who left adventuring behind to run the Turkish House. Still loves reminiscing about his past exploits. Can hint at the Scarab Cult's activities and offer practical advice drawn from his experience in the field. Players can also learn of the cult through conversations here. |
| Bartender 1 | Minor Scarab Cult contact | Unnamed; frequents or works the Turkish House. Can be used as a lead or informant for the party's investigation into the Scarab Cult. |
| Bartender 2 | Minor Scarab Cult contact | Unnamed; a second contact the party may encounter. Another thread to pull when investigating the cult. |

---

### The Tea House

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Layla al-Saleh | Tea House owner / innkeeper | Graceful and attentive, Layla runs a serene retreat for weary travelers. A keen observer of people, she quietly gathers information from her guests and shares it with those she trusts. Provides lodging for the night and can offer comfort and subtle wisdom to the party. |

---

### School of Ancient Knowledge

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Professor Amara | Head historian / Missing Explorers quest-giver | Runs the School of Ancient Knowledge and oversees excavation and exploration around the desert. She is in distress when the party meets her — a team of explorers she sent to a nearby termite mound has gone missing. She commissions the party to find them, rewarding each rescue with 14 gold and a Scroll of Heal. |

## Act 1 — Side Quest: The Missing Explorers

### The Western Hive — Androsphinx Statue Chamber

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| The Missing Explorers (×4) | Scholars / rescue targets | Four unnamed academics sent by Professor Amara to explore uncharted sections of the hive. They are found asleep in the final chamber, under the cursed gaze of the Androsphinx Statue. Each explorer rescued earns the party 14 gold from Professor Amara. |

## Act 1 — Side Quest: The Scarab Cult's Plan

### The Western Hive — Cult Chambers

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Cult Leader (Unnamed) | Fanatical mage-priest / Scarab Cult leader | A fanatical priest and powerful Mage who believes in the prophecy that reviving the giant ant queen will bring a new era of dominance for his followers. He leads the cult from hidden chambers in the western hive and performs a dark ritual to hatch the queen ant egg. On death he carries a magical staff; his priest followers each carry a Candle of Invocation. |

## Act 2 — The Desert Journey

### The Jinn's Maze

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| The Jinn | Mystical desert spirit / maze keeper | A powerful and enigmatic Jinn encountered during the desert journey. He forces the party into his maze one by one — each player travels 150 ft before being teleported back outside and taking damage. The Jinn alludes to a mysterious item the party will need in the future. The first piece of Ra's Sundial waits at the center of the maze. |

---

### The Ancient Ruins

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| The Djinni | Ruins guardian | Guards the central chamber of the ancient ruins, surrounded by mummy bodyguards. The party can attempt to reason with him to gain access to the hieroglyphs that reveal the location of Seti's tomb, or they can fight their way through. Rewards include the tomb's location and a magical ring found in the inner chamber. |

---

### Gynosphinx's Colosseum

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| The Gynosphinx | Colosseum master / optional combatant | A powerful Gynosphinx who transports the party to an ancient desert colosseum via sandstorm and forces them to fight three escalating waves of enemies for her amusement. After the waves, the party may fight her or convince her they are worthy to continue. She rewards exceptional fighters with the greatsword *Flame Tongue*. |

---

### The Lost Forest *(optional encounter)*

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| The Dryad | Forest spirit / optional ally | An ancient forest spirit who emerges from the trees when the party rests in the hidden forest valley. Her skin has the hue of aged wood and she is attended by two unicorns and two treants. She speaks of Anubis's rising and the threat to the natural world. If treated with respect she offers the forest as a sanctuary and a source of counsel. If the encounter turns hostile she and her companions will fight. |

## Act 3 — The Tomb of Seti

### Outside the Tomb

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| The Couatl | Celestial guardian / moral challenge | A Couatl stationed at the entrance to Seti's tomb, genuinely believing the party's mission does not serve the greater good. It does not wish to reveal too much for fear of angering the gods, but can be persuaded to stand aside — the key is mentioning the Jinn who bestowed the first piece of Ra's Sundial. Fighting the Couatl is possible but not recommended. |

---

### The Tomb — Lower Chamber (Boss)

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Pharaoh Seti (Sekhemket) | Undead pharaoh / Act 3 boss | The spirit of the ancient Pharaoh Seti, who has awakened in search of his lost artifact, the Ankh of Eternity. His stirring is the source of the unnatural sandstorms plaguing Saqaar. He is fought in the lower chamber of his tomb alongside his loyal undead priest slaves. Upon his defeat, a second piece of Ra's Sundial is uncovered. The Book of Vile Darkness is also found in his chamber. |

---

## Act 4 — The Prophecy Unfolds

> Act 4 returns the party to Saqaar. No new characters are introduced; Imam Zakariya al-Masri and the City Council are revisited. See their entries under Act 1.

---

## Act 5 — The Androsphinx's Lair

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| The Androsphinx | Ancient guardian / optional ally | A legendary and ancient Androsphinx who guards the final piece of Ra's Sundial in his lair. The party must fight through waves of his minions before confronting him. He may yield the Sundial's final piece willingly if sufficiently impressed, or he will fight to the death. Either way — willingly or with his dying breath — he reveals the path to the Temple of Anubis through the Lost City of Thoth. |

---

## Act 6 — Temple of Anubis

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Anubis's High Priest | Ritual caster / secondary antagonist | An unnamed high priest who has already completed the summoning ritual by the time the party reaches the inner sanctum — triggering Anubis's materialization. He is a secondary target during the final battle; disrupting him or the ritual elements may help weaken Anubis. He wears a special magical cloak. |
| Anubis | God of the Underworld / final boss | The risen god of death himself, partially materialized when the party arrives. He commands legions of undead and wields powerful necromantic magic. The Sun Dial of Ra can be used during the fight to momentarily trap Anubis in different points in time, weakening his connection to the mortal realm and creating openings for critical strikes. Upon his defeat he is banished back to the underworld. |

## Act Misc — Town of Zarhira

### The Sultan's Rest

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Fadila al-Nisr | Inn manager | A sharp-minded, warm middle-aged woman who manages the Sultan's Rest, Zarhira's grandest inn. Famous for remembering the name and preferences of every guest. Her network of contacts stretches across the region — for those who know how to ask, she can arrange rare goods or hard-to-find information, all conducted with absolute discretion. |

---

### The Desert Rose Tavern

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Hassan "The Bull" Farouk | Tavern owner / former mercenary | A tall, broad-shouldered former mercenary who now runs the Desert Rose Tavern. Booming laugh, generous spirit, and a firm hand when it comes to keeping the peace. His past is full of adventure and he is always willing to swap stories over a drink — and may offer the occasional job to those who seem capable. Does not tolerate trouble in his establishment. |

---

### The Oasis Bazaar

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Layla al-Farid | Jeweler | Runs a stall in the Oasis Bazaar renowned for delicate silver and gemstone work. Her hands are deft and her craftsmanship famous throughout the region. She weaves stories into each piece, claiming they carry the energy of the desert's mysteries. She also deals in rare and magical amulets for those with deeper pockets. |
| Ibrahim | Spice merchant | A weathered, well-traveled merchant whose stall is a feast of aromas — saffron, cumin, cinnamon, and herbs gathered from across the desert and beyond. He is a master of concoctions and can supply not just culinary spices but remedies and potions made from rare herbs. He loves entertaining customers with stories of his travels and far-off lands. |
