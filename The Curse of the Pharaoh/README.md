# The Curse of the Pharaoh

A D&D 5e campaign set in an Egyptian-inspired desert world, spanning 6 acts from a merchant city besieged by sandstorms to a climactic battle against the god of the underworld.

---

## Overview

The adventurers are recruited by three foreign merchants — **Amunet, Kasir, and Nadir** — to save the great city of **Saqaar**. What begins as a hunt for a stolen artifact escalates into a race to prevent the return of the god **Anubis** and the end of an age.

**Tone:** Ancient mystery, desert survival, puzzle-solving, epic mythology  
**Setting:** The city of Saqaar and the vast desert surrounding it  
**Themes:** Resurrection, prophecy, time, divine power

## The City of Saqaar

Saqaar was built inside a colossal ancient ant hive carved from bedrock — a mountain-sized structure with winding tunnels, grand halls, and intricate carvings. Once the seat of a vast empire, it now survives as a thriving center of trade and commerce at the edge of a great desert.

### Act 1 — City of Saqaar

**Inciting Incident:** Unnatural sandstorms plague Saqaar, believed to be the curse of the undead pharaoh **Seti**, who seeks his lost artifact — the **Ankh of Eternity**.

**Main Quest Assignment:** The ruling council hires the party to find the Ankh and end the curse. They point the adventurers toward ancient desert ruins.

#### The Market, Saqaar
![Saqaar Market](assets/maps/desert_bazzar.jpg)

#### Side Quests

**1. The Lost Caravan**
Merchant Nazir's caravan has gone missing on the road from Zarhira. The party must track it through the desert, survive a bandit ambush, and rescue the survivors from a hidden hideout.
- *Reward:* 100 gold + **Ring of Fire Elemental Command**
- *Bonus:* Nazir becomes an ally and offers market discounts

#### The Lost Caravan — Bandit Ambush & Hideout
![Desert Bandit Canyon](assets/maps/desert_bandit_canyon.jpg) ![Desert Bandit Fort](assets/maps/desert_bandit_fort.webp)

**2. The Scarab Cult's Plan**
Whispers in the market hint at a secretive cult worshipping giant scarabs. The cult seeks to find and hatch a legendary queen ant egg hidden deep in the ancient hive, believing it will usher in a new age of prosperity. The party must infiltrate the cult's chambers in the western hive and stop the ritual.
- *Reward:* Magical staff (from the high priest) + **Candle of Invocation** (per priest)
- *Creatures:* Giant Spider, Cult Fanatic, Phase Spider, Mage, Darkmantle

#### The Scarab Cult — Tunnels & Temple
![Scarab Cult Tunnels](assets/images/scarab_cult_tunnels.jpg) ![Scarab Cult Temple](assets/maps/scarab_cult_temple_saqqar.jpg) ![Scarab Cult Tunnels Map](assets/maps/scarab_cult_tunnels_saqaar.jpg)

**3. The Missing Explorers**
Professor Amara's team of scholars went missing in uncharted hive tunnels. The party must navigate three puzzle rooms to find them:

| Room | Challenge |
|---|---|
| **The Echoing Hall** | A long hallway where certain sounds cause vibrations in the walls — find the right melody to open a hidden door |
| **The Bridge over the Abyss** | A vast abyss with no visible crossing — activate pressure plates around the edge in the correct order |
| **The Room of Statues** | Six deity statues (Shamash, Nergal, Sin, Ishtar, Ea, Marduk) must be oriented correctly based on their mythological significance |
| **Androsphinx Statue Chamber** | A statue emits a sleeping curse (Con save DC 13) — find a way to disable it to free the explorers |

- *Reward:* Magical shield + Scroll of Heal + 14g per rescued explorer

### Act 2 — The Desert Journey

The party ventures into the harsh desert toward ancient ruins.

- **Desert Challenges:** Extreme heat, sandstorms, giant scorpions, sand elementals, wandering spirits
- **The Mysterious Jinn:** A Jinn forces the party through an individual maze — each player takes 5 turns (150 ft) before being transported out, taking damage, and allowing the next player to enter. Players must coordinate order and decide whether to be replaced mid-maze or start fresh. The Jinn alludes to a mysterious item they will need — the **first piece of the Sundial of Ra**.
- **Ancient Ruins:** Hieroglyphs reveal the location of Seti's tomb. The party must defeat the Djinni guardian to read them.
- **The Valley of Whispering Dunes:** Between the forgotten ruins and the desert oasis lies a treacherous valley containing the **Skull Cave** — the lair of **Sserath**, an Adult Brass Dragon. Sserath is garrulous and curious, but volatile after years of isolation and exposure to Seti's encroaching curse. The party may talk their way through (DC 14 Persuasion) or face him in a **CR 13 boss fight**. His hoard includes 1,400 gp, a Potion of Fire Resistance, and a Necklace of Fireballs.
- **Gynosphinx's Colosseum:** A sandstorm transports the party to an ancient colosseum where a Gynosphinx commands them to fight 3 waves of enemies before either fighting the Gynosphinx or convincing her of their worthiness.

### Act 3 — The Tomb of Seti

- **Outside the Tomb:** A **Couatl** guards the entrance and attempts to turn the party back, warning their deeds may not serve the greater good. The party may be able to avoid combat if they can prove their intentions are just.
- **Entering the Tomb:** The party navigates monster-filled corridors to find the lower chambers.
- **Final Confrontation:** An epic battle against Seti's spirit and his undead servants in the lower chamber.
- **Resolution:** Seti's spirit is laid to rest. The party recovers the **second piece of the Sundial of Ra** from the tomb. They return to Saqaar as heroes.
- **Hook:** The victory is short-lived — Seti's awakening has triggered a greater prophecy.

### Act 4 — The Prophecy Unfolds

**New Threat:** Seti's return has set in motion the coming of **Anubis**, who will bring an age of darkness. The party learns this through visions granted by the two pieces of the Sundial of Ra.

**Ra's Sundial:** To stop Anubis, the party must reach the **Temple of Time** in the heart of the desert and perform a ritual using the complete **Sundial of Ra** — but the third and final piece must first be found.

**New Lead:** The Priests of Ra reveal that the third piece is protected by an **Androsphinx** at a desert oasis.

**The Androsphinx:** The party must delve into the Androsphinx's tomb and maze to defeat him. His lair contains the **final piece of the Sundial of Ra**. ### Act 6 — The Temple of Time

**Entering the Temple:** The Temple of Time uses time itself as a puzzle. The party uses the complete Sundial of Ra to manipulate time and overcome obstacles. Anubis's growing presence is felt throughout.

**The Failed Ritual:** The party arrives too late — a high priest has already begun the ritual to summon Anubis. The god of the underworld begins to materialize.

**Final Confrontation:** The party faces **Anubis**, god of the underworld — commanding legions of undead and wielding powerful necromantic magic. Strategies include targeting the high priest or disrupting the remaining ritual elements.

**The Turning Point:** By using the Sundial of Ra, the party can momentarily trap Anubis in different points in time, weakening his connection to the mortal realm and opening windows for critical strikes.

**Victory:** Anubis is banished to the underworld. The temple crumbles. The party escapes just in time.

**Resolution:** Back in Saqaar, the party is celebrated as legendary saviors of the desert. The Sundial of Ra is entrusted to the priests of Ra for safekeeping.
