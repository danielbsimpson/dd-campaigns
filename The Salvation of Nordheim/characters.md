## Act -1 — Woodvost

The campaign's starting town. The party gathers here before being approached by Bjorn Ironclad.

### Town Leadership

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Melissa Sutton | Village ruler | Governs Woodvost's population of 286. Unknown to most, she is secretly controlled by the local thieves guild. Shares a surname with Godfrey Sutton. |
| Ella Wilberforce | Sheriff | Commands Woodvost's defense. A militia of 28 can be mustered under her direction. Responsible for the village's day-to-day security. |

---

### The Giant Fox (Tavern)

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Guy Clayden | Tavern owner | A small but kind man who loves his town. Happy to donate use of the rental cottage and will offer bunkbeds, a discount on dinner and lodging, or other generosity if coaxed — perhaps in exchange for a song or good company. |
| Arthur Grim | Cook | Hard at work in the kitchen, preparing multiple dishes at any given time. Found behind the fire pit at the center of the tavern. |

---

### Minotaur's Buckle (Blacksmith)

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Rosie Hayley | Blacksmith | A tall woman with muscular arms and short hair, often found hammering arrowheads at the anvil. Her shop is guarded by Sylvester — a large, friendly black-and-gray cat about twice the size of an ordinary housecat. Carries standard weapons, armor, a Blink Hatchet, and a Rusted Shield. |

---

### Fate & Vials (Alchemist)

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Helen England | Alchemist | Specializes in gathering and crafting potions of healing. Has a small stock she sells at a slight discount; others cost 50 gp. Can also craft Potions of Climbing, Water Breathing, and Fire Resistance (100 gp each, one day turnaround, half paid upfront). |
| Jane Brandon | Shop patron | A minor NPC found seated and having tea inside Fate & Vials. |

## Act 0 — The Introduction

### The Giant Fox Tavern

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Bjorn Ironclad | Viking warrior / main quest-giver | Born under the Northern Lights in Nordheim, raised by a family of renowned warriors. Tall and broad-shouldered, he wears intricately etched armor with a fur-lined cloak, and carries piercing blue eyes like glacial ice. He arrives at the tavern to recruit adventurers for King Ragnar. He is deeply loyal to Nordheim and scarred by years of battle. He possesses a boat moored at Hafnheim, ready to sail north. He will travel alongside the party as companion and guide. |

## Hafnheim

A bustling coastal port town the party passes through on their way north. Plagued by dragon attacks and bandit raids.

### Gullveig's Hearth (Mead Hall)

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Reik Ironbeard | Mead hall owner / community hub | Hardy and jovial, with a reddish-brown beard and a booming laugh as legendary as the mead he pours. Born to a line of blacksmiths and sailors, he chose warmth and camaraderie over the forge. He once witnessed a dragon's silhouette in the moonlit sky and has been vigilant ever since, gathering traveler tales about the creature. Suspects the Blackshore Raiders are connected to an old castle to the north. A key source of information about both side quests. |

---

### Skjold's Rest (Longhouse / Inn)

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Astrid Stormshield | Inn owner | Striking and commanding, with flowing auburn hair and ocean-deep eyes. A survivor of a treacherous shipwreck, she grew into a symbol of resilience in Hafnheim. Her longhouse is a gathering point for sailors, travelers, and those seeking counsel. She believes the old castle to the north holds answers to the dragon and bandit troubles, and quietly urges adventurers toward action. Offers standard and deluxe rooms. |

---

### Ironbeard's Forge (Blacksmith)

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Durg Ironbeard | Blacksmith | A burly man with a beard as dark as the iron he shapes. His calloused hands and steady, precise craft reflect years at the forge. He greets visitors with a knowing nod. Sells a Dwarven Thrower, warhammers, shields, smith's tools, and basic equipment. |

---

### Arcane Enclaves (Enchanter's Emporium)

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Aeliana Moonshadow | Wizard / enchanter | A reclusive wizard draped in elegant robes, with eyes that shimmer with knowledge and curiosity. Runs Arcane Enclaves, a shop filled with magical trinkets, scrolls, and potent potions tucked in a discreet corner of Hafnheim. Prices are steep, but the rewards are significant for those seeking powerful magic. Sells healing potions, Alchemist's Fire, Potions of Fire Breath, Potions of Growth, and a Scroll of Feather Fall. |

---

### Temple of the Allfather

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Temple Priests (unnamed) | Priests of Odin | Oversee the grand hilltop temple dedicated to Odin. Can offer blessings, sacred relics, and divine guidance to the party before they set out north. Specific names not established in the campaign text. |

### Hafnheim — Side Quest: The Blackshore Raiders

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Captain Skyraven (Grimm Blackthorn) | Bandit leader / side quest antagonist | The cunning and charismatic leader of the Blackshore Raiders, known as "Captain Skyraven" by reputation. His true name is Grimm Blackthorn. He dresses in dark leather armor adorned with raven-feathered trinkets and commands from a fortified tower deep in the marshlands. A masterful tactician who uses ambushes and hit-and-run tactics, keeping his band fiercely loyal. CR 5. On defeat his locked chest yields 1500 gp and 3 Black Pearl gems. |

## Frostgate

A small coastal town at the northern edge of the known world — the gateway to Nordheim itself.

### The Frosted Mug (Tavern)

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Ingrid Oakheart | Tavern owner | A stout and hearty woman with a weathered face and a welcoming smile. She moves with purpose through the tavern, her eyes holding a glint of wisdom from years behind the bar. She embodies the spirit of camaraderie that the Frosted Mug is built around — a place where tales are spun and friendships are forged. |

---

### The Frostgate Rest (Inn)

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Sigurd Froststrider | Inn owner / adventurer-turned-innkeeper | A hardy and seasoned adventurer with a weathered face and piercing blue eyes. After years roaming the frozen north — including surviving a wolf attack and a treacherous avalanche — he settled in Frostgate. He knows the town's mysteries intimately: the frozen lighthouse and the strange iceberg that appeared overnight. He actively points adventurers toward both side quests, believing their resolution is key to the town's safety. |

---

### Nordic Traders (General Store)

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Eirik Skaldhammer | General store owner | A robust figure with a braided beard and intense blue eyes, clad in sturdy furs with a finely crafted axe at his side. Warm, laughing, and knowledgeable about his wares. Sells winter gear, camping supplies, provisions, maps, and basic adventuring goods. |

---

### Fireforge's Forge (Blacksmith)

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Brogar Ironforge | Dwarf blacksmith | A sturdy dwarf with broad shoulders and weathered hands, passionate about his craft. Since the lighthouse froze over and commerce slowed, he has been unable to source new goods. He can only offer repair services and has a knack for mechanical workings — valuable for the lighthouse side quest. |

---

### Frostgate — Side Quest: The Frozen Lighthouse

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Olaf | Old fisherman / quest-giver | An elderly local fisherman who believes that restoring the ancient lighthouse on the nearby ice-covered island would help guide ships safely into Frostgate's harbor. He approaches the party and asks them to brave the frozen journey and light the beacon once more. |

---

### Frostgate — Side Quest: The Mysterious Iceberg

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Elinor | Local scholar / quest-giver | A Frostgate scholar fascinated by a massive iceberg that appeared off the coast after a recent blizzard. Legends say an ancient chamber within holds the "Frostheart Crystal." She approaches the party to help decipher the elemental puzzle locking the chamber and unlock its secrets. |

## Nordheim Kingdom

The besieged Viking kingdom at the heart of the campaign. Once a thriving realm, it now endures under the shadow of monstrous attacks.

### The Great Hall

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| King Ragnar Frostbeard | King of Nordheim / main quest patron | A legendary warrior and wise ruler who greets the party with a booming voice and deep gratitude. He explains that the Frostfang Clan — cannibal warriors disguised to mimic the Frostclaw Behemoth — have been raiding his towns. He initially mistakes the disguised men for the actual Hrimfang spawn. After the first battle in the great hall, he stands united with the party, promises them the legendary sword *Frostbite*, and calls for celebration and preparation for the true battle ahead. He personally delivers the reward once the Frostbane Matriarch is defeated. |

---

### Aurora Anvil (Blacksmith)

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Ragnhild Ironhand | Blacksmith | A stout and seasoned blacksmith with intricate runic tattoos on her arms. Works in mournful silence with a sense of quiet determination, each hammer strike carrying the weight of the siege's toll. The shop's walls and floors shimmer with colors reminiscent of the Northern Lights. Sells chain mail, frost-resistant armor variants, and studded leather. |

---

### Shieldmaiden's Armory (Weapons & Armor)

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Astrid Ironheart | Weapons shop owner | A stoic proprietor who stands behind the counter inspecting and mending armor with quiet resolve. Her shop walls bear the scars of battle but remain stocked with shields, helmets, greatswords, battleaxes, and a Viking Battleaxe. Her presence evokes the indomitable spirit of Nordheim's warriors. |

---

### The Mead Hall (Tavern)

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Sigrid Aleheart | Mead hall owner | A welcoming figure behind the bar, pouring hearty mugs of ale and mead with a genuine smile. She manages a hall that has emerged from the siege with renewed purpose — scarred but vibrant, full of travelers and warriors sharing stories of triumph. |

---

### Valkyrie's Apothecary

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Ingrid Windrider | Apothecary owner | An enigmatic woman in a hooded cloak, moving silently among dimly lit shelves of ancient potions and remedies. Labels are written in cryptic runes. Her apothecary carries a mysterious aura — whispers suggest her remedies unlock secrets and visions. Sells the Frostfire Elixir, Valkyrie's Tincture, Raven's Bane Salve, Whisperwind Poultice, and standard healing potions. |

---

### Raven's Rest Inn & The Wise Raven's Bookshop

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Eirik Ravenshadow | Inn and bookshop owner | Runs both the Raven's Rest Inn and the adjacent Wise Raven's Bookshop. He surveys the inn's common room with a sharp, raven-like gaze, having mended its walls from battle damage with care. In the bookshop, he presides over ancient tomes and scrolls in quiet scholarly reverence. He is a man of few words but deep knowledge — a reliable source for lore and rare written materials. |

---

### Odin's Blessing Temple

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| High Priestess Freya | Temple high priestess | Steward of Odin's Blessing Temple, which has remained untouched by the siege's chaos. A place of profound reverence and tranquility under her care. Worshippers kneel in quiet prayer and make offerings. She oversees the sale of blessed talismans, candles, prayer beads of Thor, and Vials of Sacred Water. |

---

### Nordheim — Side Quest: The Haunting of Nordholm Abbey

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Malevolent Necromancer | Ghost / side quest boss | An ancient and powerful necromancer's spirit, trapped between realms in the graveyard of Nordholm Abbey. It manifests as a blurred, translucent spectral figure in tattered robes, commanding a horde of risen skeletal monks and restless spirits. It hisses warnings and gestures graves into life. Defeating it and freeing its tormented soul is the only way to end the haunting. CR 6. |

## Valkyriehaven

A newly revitalized port town that serves as the campaign's final destination. The party is escorted here by King Ragnar as a reward following the defeat of the Frostbane Matriarch.

### Valkyrie's Rest (Tavern / Inn)

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Bartender (unnamed) | Innkeeper | A burly man with a braided beard behind the newly built oak bar, expertly filling mugs of frothy ale. The post-campaign celebration takes place here, with a guarantee of lodging for three nights courtesy of the King. |
| Mysterious Hooded Figure | Unknown / post-campaign tease | As the party celebrates in the Valkyrie's Rest, the door is thrown open by a mysterious figure — noticeably shorter than 4 feet, unlike Bjorn's commanding entrance months before. Their arrival mirrors the opening of the campaign and hints at the next adventure yet to come. Their identity is not revealed. |
