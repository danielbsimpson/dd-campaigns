# La Llorona

A D&D 5e campaign set in a Latin American-inspired river valley, designed for parties of levels 13–15. A grief-consumed spirit haunts the river that runs through a dying town — and the truth buried beneath her weeping is darker than anyone still living dares to say aloud.

> For full scene descriptions, encounter details, and DM notes, see [La Llorona.txt](La%20Llorona.txt).

---

## Overview

A desperate council member rides three days to find help because her own town refuses to ask for it. The river town of **Río Oscuro** has been suffering for three months — children vanishing, a weeping woman in white seen at the water's edge after dark, and a cover-up running three years deep into the town's leadership. Behind it all is **La Llorona** — the spirit of Esperanza Vargas de Reyes — who is not simply a monster. She is a woman who died asking for justice and never received it.

**Tone:** Gothic horror, mystery, emotional confrontation, moral weight
**Setting:** The town of Río Oscuro and the cursed Río de las Sombras river valley
**Themes:** Grief and guilt, truth versus silence, the cost of justice, the line between villain and victim

---

## The Town of Río Oscuro

Río Oscuro is a mid-sized river town built along the banks of the **Río de las Sombras** — the River of Shadows — a wide, slow-moving waterway once famous for its silver fish and prosperous trade. Now the water runs cold in midsummer. The ferry hasn't crossed in two months. Children are warned never to go near the water after dark. Three have already not come back.

## Campaign Structure

### Act 0 — The Messenger
**Consuela Vargas** rides to find the party — three days, alone, with her own money — because the Alcalde refuses to issue a formal call for help. She brings a locked iron box addressed to *"whoever is brave enough to read this when the river finally speaks."* Three children are dead. More will follow.

---

### Act 1 — The Town of Río Oscuro
The party arrives to a town closing early, going quiet, calling children indoors before sunset. Father Domingo is the first real ally. The Alcalde is polite, unhelpful, and afraid. The investigation is driven by social encounters and evidence-gathering across four side quests that build toward the truth of what happened three years ago.

#### Side Quests

| Quest | Summary | Enemies | Reward |
|---|---|---|---|
| **The Locked Box** | Decode a hidden notary account exposing a three-year-old murder cover-up | None — pure investigation | Full documented evidence; leverage over Vásquez |
| **The Survivor** | Earn the trust of Tomas, only known La Llorona survivor; he carries her message | None | La Llorona's true motive revealed; moral stakes reframed |
| **The East Bank Cemetery** | Discover Esperanza's disturbed grave, the unmarked children's graves, and Mateo's identity | Ghast ×2 | Esperanza's identity; Father Domingo's binding prayer |
| **The Night Watch** | Rescue a militia watcher from the Weeping Willow Grove; clear La Llorona's undead followers | Will-o'-Wisp ×3, Specter ×4 | Captain Dávila as ally; 200 gold; access to restricted records |

---

### Act 2 — The Shape of the Lie
Evidence from Act 1 converges on one story: **Alcalde Vásquez** had Mateo Reyes — a boatwright who knew about his financial crimes — killed three years ago. The official verdict was drowning. Physical proof is locked in a chest under the ferry dock: the original evidence ledger Mateo was taking to the governor.

The party must retrieve it from 15 feet of cold, dark river water guarded by Water Weirds and the Ghost of the notary who buried the truth.

- *Enemies:* Water Weird ×2, Ghost (Jorge Espinoza — not hostile if approached peacefully)
- *Reward:* Mateo's evidence ledger; standing to compel the governor's intervention

---

### Act 3 — The Sunken Hacienda
Esperanza's family home — half-submerged, grief-soaked, and filled with the residual presence of everything she lost. The party enters to find the final piece they need: **Mateo's letter**, written the night before he died, naming the Alcalde and asking for his family to be remembered.

| Room | Encounter | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Entry Hall** | Shadow ×3 | DC 13 Religion to disperse with Sacred Flame without full combat |
| **Children's Room** | Wraith (the two children, fused) | Not hostile by default; speaking with it yields context and a mechanical buff vs. La Llorona's Wail |
| **The Study** | None | Mateo's letter is here; locked drawer DC 15; magically preserved |
| **Flooded Basement** | Water Weird ×1, Revenant (Mateo) | Revenant stands down if shown the ledger or letter; cannot be permanently killed here |

- *Key Item:* Mateo's letter — required for the peaceful resolution of Act 5

---

### Act 4 — The Reckoning
With all evidence in hand, the party compels Alcalde Vásquez's public confession. Captain Dávila enforces the process. Father Domingo performs the **Rite of Naming and Rest** at the cemetery — naming the children's graves, reading the truth aloud, with a living witness present.

The Rite changes the nature of Act 5. La Llorona will still fight. But she can now be reached.

The approach to the river afterward is blocked by an escalated wave of grief-drawn undead.

- *Enemies:* Wight ×2, Ghost ×2, Ghast ×3

---

### Act 5 — La Llorona
The confrontation takes place at the **Weeping Willow Grove** at midnight. The air is fifteen degrees colder than the surrounding land. The weeping can be heard from five hundred feet away. La Llorona rises from the river at initiative +20.

**La Llorona's Deathbound trait** means she cannot be reduced below 1 HP unless the Rite of Naming was completed. During combat, the party may read Mateo's letter aloud as an action — if all three peaceful conditions are met, she drops the trait and can be defeated fully.

**Two endings are possible:**

| Ending | Conditions | Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| **Requiem (Peaceful)** | Rite completed + living witness + Mateo's letter read during combat | La Llorona falls into the river; river warms; silver fish return; the weeping stops |
| **Force (Hard)** | Conditions not met | La Llorona can be banished but not destroyed; haunting ends but resurfaces elsewhere within a year |

| Creature | CR | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| La Llorona (Esperanza) | 13 | Custom boss; Deathbound, River Empowerment, Mourning Wail (DC 18), Lament Aura |
| Summoned Zombie / Ghoul | 1/4–1 | Summoned via Legendary Action up to 4 active |

---

### Act 6 — Aftermath
The governor's representative arrives within a week. Vásquez is removed and faces trial. The children's graves are properly marked. The party is honored with a festival held in their name — and in Esperanza's. The town chooses to remember rather than forget.

If the peaceful ending was achieved, Tomas walks to the river's edge alone that evening — without fear for the first time — and floats a lantern.
