### The Jinn's Maze

The Jinn is an encounter rather than a traditional combat. Each player enters the maze one at a time, traveling 150 ft before being transported back outside — taking damage from the Jinn in the process. The cycle continues until someone reaches the center and claims the first piece of Ra's Sundial.

| Creature | CR | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| The Jinn | — | Non-standard encounter; deals damage to each player as they exit the maze |

## Act 5 — The Androsphinx's Lair

The Androsphinx can be reasoned with — he may hand over the final piece of the Sundial willingly if the party impresses him, or he will fight to the death.

| Creature | CR | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Androsphinx Minions | varies | Encountered before and after the Androsphinx retreats to his lair |
| [Androsphinx](https://www.dndbeyond.com/monsters/androsphinx) | 17 | Ancient and powerful sphinx; guardian of the Sundial's final piece |

> Whether he yields peacefully or is defeated, the Androsphinx reveals the path to the Temple of Anubis through the Lost City of Thoth.

## Act 6 — Temple of Anubis

The final confrontation. The party navigates three chambers before facing the risen god.

| Creature | CR | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Undead Legion | varies | Anubis commands legions of undead throughout the temple |
| Anubis's High Priest | — | Has already completed the summoning ritual; a secondary target in the battle |
| Anubis | — | The God of the Underworld; final boss. The Sundial of Ra can be used to momentarily trap him in different points in time during the fight |

**Reward:** Magical cloak (worn by the High Priest)
