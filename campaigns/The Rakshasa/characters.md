## Act 0 — The Call

### The Envoy

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| The Envoy (unnamed) | Messenger / quest hook | An exhausted figure, travelling light and without military escort, who arrives at the party's current location bearing a sealed letter from Captain Priya Deva. The letter reads: *"Vethara is not well. Its sickness comes from within and wears familiar faces. I cannot trust anyone already inside these walls. Come quickly, and come quietly."* The envoy conveys the advance payment and the instructions to enter Vethara as ordinary travelers — not as champions. The Rakshasa must not know it is being hunted. The envoy's name and fate are left to the DM's discretion. |

## Act 1 — The City of Vethara

### The Crimson Lotus Inn

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Captain Priya Deva | City guard commander / primary ally | The party's anchor in Vethara and the one who hired them. She lives under constant professional pressure — her investigation into the city's changes has been stonewalled at every turn by the Grand Vizier. She meets the party privately at the inn after dark and lays out what she knows: half a dozen nobles and officials have visibly "changed" over the past two years; three priests of Indra were murdered last month; children in the Sunken Ward have been disappearing for six weeks. She cannot prove any of it is connected — yet. Resourceful, sharp, and genuinely concerned for her city, Priya is the party's most reliable source of official intelligence throughout the entire campaign and ultimately leads the cleanup of the fiend network after Act 5. |

---

### The Palace of a Thousand Lamps

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Maharaja Vardhan | Ruler of Vethara | The well-loved ruler of Vethara — increasingly isolated, distant, and issuing vague or contradictory proclamations with each passing moon. Those who have known him for decades whisper that he no longer seems himself. He may already have been replaced or heavily manipulated by the time the party arrives. In Act 3, the party must get evidence in front of him without Vikramasura's control filtering it first. Once the Vizier is unmasked, the Maharaja hosts the grand ceremony at the Temple of Indra that closes the campaign, awarding the party with formal titles, land grants, and estates in Vethara. |
| Grand Vizier Kiran | The Maharaja's most trusted advisor | To all appearances, Grand Vizier Kiran is the calm, measured, and indispensable right hand of Maharaja Vardhan — a man of impeccable loyalty, vast knowledge of court procedure, and quiet authority. In reality, Kiran is Vikramasura (see below). The party should not learn this immediately. The Vizier gatekeeps all access to the Maharaja, systematically stonewalls Priya's investigations, and works quickly to discredit the party if they start pulling too many threads. His handwriting appears on Infernal communications found in the underground vault in Act 2. |
| Vikramasura | Ancient Rakshasa / main villain | The true identity behind Grand Vizier Kiran — an ancient, tiger-headed demon of terrible cunning, with its hands fitted on backwards, who has spent decades hollowing out Vethara from within. It is patient, utterly without mercy, and deeply contemptuous of mortals. When unmasked by the consecrated incense in Act 3, it does not panic — it taunts the party, declares the ritual nearly complete, and tears open a dimensional rift into the jungle rather than fight in a losing position. In Act 5 it is fully itself for the first time, standing without disguise at the center of its summoning circle. It regenerates 10 HP per turn while any ritual circles remain intact and is immune to spells of 6th level or lower. CR 13. |

### The Scholar's Quarter

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Yogi Ananda | Ancient sage / lore source / key ally | An ancient and eccentric sage living in retirement in the Scholar's Quarter. One of the very few people in all of Vethara who understands what a Rakshasa truly is — its nature, its weaknesses, and how to unmask one. He is indispensable for making sense of the stolen codex pages recovered from the Temple of Indra quest, which reveal the ritual's final phase when brought to him. He also provides the party with a **Consecrated Incense** — a single-use item whose smoke dispels magical disguises and illusions within its area, and the key to unmasking the Vizier in Act 3. In the resolution, he quietly pulls the party aside to warn them that Vikramasura belongs to a lineage, and that others of that lineage will now know what happened in Vethara. |

---

### The Spice Exchange

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Nalini | Spice merchant / first quest-giver | A spice merchant whose husband Rajan vanished weeks ago after arranging a late-night meeting with a new buyer for a large shipment. She approaches the party at the Spice Exchange and asks them to find him — she knows only that something was wrong about the buyer and that Rajan wouldn't simply disappear on his own. Completing her quest earns 500 gold and a Necklace of Fireballs, and transforms Siva from a potential contact into an active informant. |
| Rajan | Nalini's missing husband | A merchant being held captive in the warehouse district by the Cambion's smuggling ring — kept as leverage to ensure the Spice Exchange causes no trouble. He is alive. Once freed, he provides the party with evidence linking the smugglers directly to the palace and confirms the nature of what was being moved through his shipment. |

---

### The Night Bazaar

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Siva | Thief, fence, and underworld informant | A thief and fence operating out of the Night Bazaar — Vethara's black market after dark. Initially a cautious, self-interested actor, Siva becomes a committed informant if the party demonstrates genuine goodwill by protecting the city's people rather than exploiting them. He is an optional contact in the Merchant's Thread quest, can point the party toward the storm drain entrance for the Missing Children quest, and in Act 2 is the key to accessing the palace supply chain without triggering an alert through the Bone Devil's Vault. He will only commit to helping with the vault if the party has completed at least one Act 1 side quest in a way that protected rather than exploited the city. |

---

### The Temple of Indra

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Chief Priest Arjuna | High priest of Indra / under direct attack | Head of Vethara's primary religious center. Three of his temple brothers have been murdered — each found drained of life at their personal shrine, offerings consumed, sacred texts stolen. He approaches the party for help investigating the killings. The trail leads to the Night Hag coven beneath the city, who are harvesting souls for Vikramasura's preparation ritual. Once the coven is defeated and the stolen codex pages recovered, Arjuna directs the party to Yogi Ananda for analysis. He consecrates the party at the final ceremony in the campaign's resolution, presenting them before the gods in Vethara's name. |

---

### The Military Barracks

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| General Abhaya (Nycaloth impostor) | Army commander — impostor | Not the real general. A **[Nycaloth](https://www.dndbeyond.com/monsters/nycaloth)** (CR 9) has replaced General Abhaya and is issuing unusual orders — rotating proven officers out of sensitive posts, replacing them with unfamiliar faces, and behaving with a cold precision entirely at odds with the general's known character. If not exposed before the Maharaja's upcoming coronation ceremony, a staged assassination attempt will consolidate Vikramasura's control over the city in a single night. Defeating or exposing the Nycaloth earns the party General Abhaya's military ring and the Ring of Mind Shielding. |
| General Abhaya (real) | Imprisoned army commander | The genuine commander of Vethara's army — loyal, known to his officers, and very much alive, imprisoned in a hidden cell beneath the military barracks where the Nycaloth stored him. Once freed, he provides his military ring (granting command of city guard resources for direct palace access in Act 3) and stands alongside Captain Priya during the post-Act 5 cleanup of the remaining fiend network. |

## Act 2 — The Shadow Network

> No new named characters are introduced in Act 2. The party works through existing contacts — Siva, Priya, and Yogi Ananda — to connect the threads from Act 1 and access the Bone Devil's Vault. The vault's ledgers name every replaced official in Vethara but these figures are not individually named in the campaign text.

---

## Act 3 — The Palace of Whispers

> Act 3 revolves around existing characters. The Maharaja, the Grand Vizier/Vikramasura, Priya, Yogi Ananda, and General Abhaya (if freed in Act 1) are the key figures. The palace also contains unnamed courtiers — some deceived, some replaced, some genuine — that the party must read and navigate in the social gauntlet at court. The DM is encouraged to name and develop a selection of these courtiers to suit the party's approach.

---

## Act 4 — The Jungle Road

### The Jungle Temple Entrance

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Guardian Naga | Bound ancient guardian / conditional ally | An ancient **[Guardian Naga](https://www.dndbeyond.com/monsters/guardian-naga)** (CR 10) that has been magically bound to the temple gate for decades against its will, forced to serve as a deterrent. It is exhausted, bitter, and deeply resentful of Vikramasura. It is not an enemy — it is a prisoner. If the party speaks with it and offers to break the binding once the Rakshasa is defeated, the Naga will stand aside and share everything it knows about the temple's interior: where the ritual circles are, the layout of all three tiers, and the fastest route to the inner sanctum. It is one of the most valuable strategic assets in the campaign if the party treats it with respect rather than hostility. |

---

## Act 5 — The Temple of Vikramasura

> No new named characters appear in Act 5. The final antagonists are Vikramasura, the summoned [Efreeti](https://www.dndbeyond.com/monsters/efreeti), and unnamed Temple Acolytes maintaining the ritual. See [creatures.md](creatures.md) for full stat blocks on all Act 5 combatants.

---

## Act 6 — Resolution

> The resolution returns to the city's core cast. Maharaja Vardhan, Captain Priya Deva, General Abhaya, Chief Priest Arjuna, and Yogi Ananda all play roles in the closing ceremony and debrief. Yogi Ananda's private warning to the party — that Vikramasura's lineage will take notice — sets the stage for any future campaign.
