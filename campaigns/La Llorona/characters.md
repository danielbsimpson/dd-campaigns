## Act 0 — The Messenger

### The Party's Current Location

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Consuela Vargas | Council member / quest giver | A young woman who has ridden three days alone to find outside help, because Alcalde Vásquez refuses to issue a formal request. She arrives with dust on her clothes, exhaustion in her face, and a locked iron box in her saddlebag addressed to *"whoever is brave enough to read this when the river finally speaks."* Sharp, pragmatic, and frightened — she does not dramatize things. She names what is happening plainly: three children are dead, a spirit is haunting the river, and the man in charge of the town is letting it continue. She offers 800 gold from her own savings plus a town council writ — she cannot offer more, and she does not pretend she can. The party's judgment of her credibility is the first meaningful character read of the campaign. |

## Act 1 — The Town of Río Oscuro

### The Church of Santa Muerte

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Father Domingo Vela | Primary ally / lore source / rite performer | An elderly priest — unhurried, precise, and careful with his words. He knows the legend of La Llorona in full, knows it did not begin as folklore, and suspects the Río Oscuro haunting has a specific local cause rather than a mythological one. He does not push; he waits for the party to ask the right questions, then answers them completely. He is the party's most reliable source of context throughout the campaign and the only person in town who can perform the Rite of Naming and Rest in Act 4. He has one condition for doing so: a living witness must be willing to speak the truth aloud. Securing that witness — almost certainly the Alcalde — is the party's burden, not his. In the resolution, he commissions a carved memorial at the river and speaks the first public words in Río Oscuro acknowledging Esperanza by name. |

---

### The Town Hall

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Alcalde Rodrigo Vásquez | Primary human antagonist / cover-up architect | The town's mayor and the man most responsible for what La Llorona has become. He is not a cartoonish villain — he is a man who made a decision three years ago that he cannot undo, surrounded himself with enough justifications that he has nearly convinced himself, and is now watching the consequences arrive from a direction he cannot control. He is polite to the party, offers nothing substantive, reframes the children's deaths as accidents, and suggests the party might be more comfortable in a safer town. His tells are subtle: he does not make eye contact when Mateo's name is mentioned; he overexplains his schedule when asked where he was three years ago; he knows La Llorona's name before the party tells it to him. When confronted with the notary's account, the ledger, and Tomas's testimony as a unified body of evidence — with Captain Dávila present to enforce the process — he breaks. His confession is not loud. It is quiet and disgusting and entirely believable. |

### The West Bank (Señora Inés's Home)

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Señora Inés Morada | Elder witness / herbalist / oldest memory in town | An elderly herbalist and midwife who has lived in Río Oscuro longer than anyone else still living. She delivered most of the town's children, including Esperanza's. She knows what happened to Mateo. She watched it happen, in so far as anyone watched — she saw the body; she knew the marks on his wrists were not from drowning; she said nothing because she was afraid and because she is human and because the Alcalde is powerful. She carries that with considerable weight. She will not volunteer information, but she will not lie if asked directly. A Persuasion check (DC 13) or a successful first Insight read (DC 12) reveals that she knows more than she is sharing. She becomes fully cooperative once the notary's account is in the party's hands — she confirms it, names what she saw, and will serve as a living witness at the Rite if the party cannot compel the Alcalde. She is a backup plan, and she knows it. |

---

### East Bank — Tomas's Home

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Tomas | Sole survivor of a La Llorona encounter / key witness | A twelve-year-old boy who was found on the riverbank two weeks ago, unconscious and soaking wet, the only person known to have seen La Llorona at close range and lived. He has not spoken more than essential words since. He will not speak near the river. He will not speak in the town hall or anywhere that feels like authority. He needs to feel genuinely safe — removed from the water-facing side of town, with people who are not trying to extract anything from him. When he finally speaks, his account is memorable: she looked at him, she spoke, and what she said was *"Tell them what happened to Mateo."* This is the campaign's clearest signal that La Llorona is not simply murderous — she is asking for something. Tomas knows this. He has been trying to figure out how to say it ever since. |
| Lucía | Tomas's mother / protective NPC | Tomas's mother — protective, exhausted, and frightened. She does not prevent the party from speaking to Tomas, but she will not leave the room, and her presence affects how Tomas responds. She is not an obstacle; she is a mother watching her son not recover and accepting any help that might work. If the party treats both of them with respect, she becomes a quiet ally — she remembers details from the nights of the disappearances and can provide a timeline the party does not otherwise have. |

### The Militia Barracks

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Private Beatriz | Militia watcher / compelled victim | A young militia member who went missing during a night watch three days before the party's arrival and was found at sunrise sitting in the Weeping Willow Grove, unresponsive, staring at the water. She is suffering the effect of La Llorona's Lament Aura — a magical compulsion drawing her toward the river — and will not respond to medicine or ordinary comfort. She does not speak, but she turns her head toward the sound of the river. Clearing the Will-o'-Wisps from the grove and providing *Remove Curse* or equivalent frees her. Once recovered, she is a grateful and detailed source of information about what the grove looks and feels like at night. |
| Captain Hector Dávila | Militia commander / conditional ally | A capable, serious officer who is loyal to the Alcalde out of institutional habit rather than deep conviction. He is ashamed that his militia cannot protect the town and quietly desperate enough to ask the party for help outside the Alcalde's oversight — his request to investigate Beatriz's situation is made privately, without permission. He responds to competence and honesty. Once Private Beatriz is recovered and the party has demonstrated both, he commits fully to working with them. His authority to compel the Alcalde's confession — given sufficient evidence — is the legal mechanism that makes the civic arc work. Without him, the party must find another way to enforce accountability. |

## Act 2 — The Shape of the Lie

### The Drowned Vault (Ferry Dock)

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Jorge Espinoza | Deceased notary / ghost / crucial witness | The town notary who discovered the ligature marks on Mateo's wrists three years ago and chose to hide his account in a locked iron box rather than file it — because he was afraid. He died of a fever eight months later, survived by his guilt. His ghost inhabits the riverbed beneath the ferry dock, near the iron chest where Mateo's physical evidence is stored. He is not hostile — he is remorseful. He will not speak (ghosts cannot naturally) but will gesture toward the chest's location and respond to *Speak with Dead* or a DC 14 Persuasion check from a party member who knows his name. Used with restraint, his Possession ability makes for a powerful storytelling moment: he briefly possesses the most empathetic party member and uses their voice to say *"I was afraid. I buried it instead of filing it. I'm sorry."* His ledger-box account is the first domino of the civic arc. |

## Act 3 — The Sunken Hacienda

### The Hacienda

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Mateo Reyes | Murdered boatwright / Revenant | The man whose death set everything in motion. He was a skilled boatwright and an ordinary person who uncovered evidence of the Alcalde's financial crimes and tried to report it to the regional governor. He was killed for it — drowned, officially; murdered, factually — before he could deliver the evidence. His spirit reconstituted as a Revenant in the flooded basement of his own home. He is not hostile to those who carry his evidence. Presenting his ledger or his letter causes the Revenant to stand down — it bows and returns to the water. If the party fights without presenting evidence, he cannot be permanently destroyed here (he reconstitutes within 24 hours). He is waiting for the truth to be told. When it finally is — the Alcalde's confession in Act 4 — the Revenant bows once and dissolves. He is the campaign's other victim, quieter than Esperanza and no less present. |

## Act 5 — La Llorona

### The Weeping Willow Grove

| Character | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Esperanza Vargas de Reyes | La Llorona — the weeping woman / final boss | The woman behind the legend. She was Mateo Reyes's wife, a mother of two, and a woman who spent the four months after her husband's murder trying to prove what happened to him. She got close — close enough that the Alcalde stopped responding. She died of grief and fever at thirty-one, certain that no one would ever say aloud what everyone already knew. Her children died months later, essentially alone. She came back. She is not a monster who chose to become this — she is a woman who received no justice, no acknowledgment, no witness, and has been trapped in that absence long enough that it has consumed her. Her attacks are expressions of grief weaponized by decades of unanswered mourning. She does not explain herself; she weeps and she drowns and she pulls things toward the water. If the party has done everything right — the Rite, the living witness, the letter read aloud — she can be laid to rest. She does not dissolve into darkness. She collapses into the river. The weeping stops. For a full stat block with all traits and abilities, see [creatures.md](creatures.md). |

## Act 6 — Resolution

> The resolution's active NPCs are all drawn from the existing cast. Consuela Vargas assumes acting Alcalde. Father Domingo leads the memorial ceremony. Captain Dávila oversees the governor's handover of authority. Tomas, in the final scene, walks to the river's edge without fear for the first time.
