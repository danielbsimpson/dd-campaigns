## Act 1 — The Town of Río Oscuro

### Quest 3: The East Bank Cemetery

#### Ghast
[D&D Beyond](https://www.dndbeyond.com/monsters/16872-ghast) | CR 2 | Medium Undead | Chaotic Evil

| AC | HP | Speed |
|---|---|---|
| 13 | 36 (8d8) | 30 ft. |

**Ability Scores:** STR 16 (+3), DEX 17 (+3), CON 10 (+0), INT 11 (+0), WIS 10 (+0), CHA 8 (−1)  
**Resistances:** Necrotic  
**Immunities:** Poison | **Condition:** Charmed, Exhaustion, Poisoned  
**Senses:** Darkvision 60 ft.

**Traits:**
- **Stench.** Any creature that starts its turn within 5 ft. must succeed on a DC 10 Constitution save or be poisoned until the start of its next turn. On success, immune to Stench for 24 hours.
- **Turning Defiance.** Advantage on saving throws against being turned.

**Actions (Multiattack — Bite + Claw):**
- **Bite.** +3 to hit, 5 ft.; Hit 12 (2d8+3) piercing. DC 10 CON or paralyzed for 1 min.
- **Claws.** +5 to hit, 5 ft.; Hit 10 (2d6+3) slashing. DC 10 CON or paralyzed for 1 min.

> **Campaign Role:** Two Ghasts lurk in the East Bank Cemetery at night, feeding on the residual grief energy emanating from Esperanza's disturbed grave. They are not controlled by La Llorona — they are opportunistic. A successful DC 13 Religion check identifies the source of their agitation; consecrating the grave site (Father Domingo can assist) disperses them without requiring a fight.

## Act 2 — The Shape of the Lie

### The Drowned Vault (Ferry Dock)

#### Water Weird *(MM licence required)*
[D&D Beyond](https://www.dndbeyond.com/monsters/water-weird) | CR 3 | Large Elemental | Neutral

| AC | HP | Speed |
|---|---|---|
| 13 | 58 (9d10+9) | Swim 60 ft. (can't move out of water) |

**Ability Scores:** STR 17 (+3), DEX 16 (+3), CON 13 (+1), INT 11 (+0), WIS 10 (+0), CHA 10 (+0)  
**Resistances:** Fire; B/P/S from nonmagical weapons  
**Immunities:** Poison, Condition: Exhaustion, Grappled, Paralyzed, Poisoned, Prone, Restrained, Unconscious  
**Senses:** Blindsight 30 ft.

**Traits:**
- **Invisible in Water.** Is invisible while fully immersed in water.
- **Water Bound.** Dies if it leaves the water it inhabits.

**Actions:**
- **Constrict.** +5 to hit, 5 ft.; Hit 13 (3d6+3) bludgeoning. Target is grappled (DC 13 STR). While grappled, target is restrained and begins to suffocate. The Water Weird can constrict only one creature at a time.

> **Campaign Role:** Two Water Weirds patrol the submerged riverbed beneath the ferry dock where Mateo's evidence chest is hidden. They are drawn to emotional resonance — the grief soaked into the chest's location — rather than serving La Llorona directly. Their invisibility underwater makes them extremely threatening when the party is already disadvantaged by the cold and the Athletics checks. Consider having one surface from the dock's shadow to surprise a party that hasn't prepared for aquatic hazards.

| AC | HP | Speed |
|---|---|---|
| 11 | 45 (10d8) | 40 ft. (Fly, hover) |

**Resistances:** Acid, Fire, Lightning, Thunder; B/P/S from nonmagical weapons

**Senses:** Darkvision 60 ft.

**Traits:**

- **Incorporeal Movement.** As above.

**Actions:**
- **Withering Touch.** +5 to hit, 5 ft.; Hit 17 (4d6+3) necrotic damage.

> **Campaign Role:** Jorge Espinoza's ghost is explicitly **not hostile** to the party — he is guilty and remorseful, not angry. He won't speak (ghosts can't naturally) but will gesture toward the chest location and respond to *Speak with Dead* or a DC 14 Persuasion check from a party member who knows his name. If the party attacks him without attempting contact, treat it as a normal Ghost encounter. Use his Possession ability only as a storytelling moment if the party is dismissive — he possesses the most empathetic party member briefly and uses their voice to say: *"I was afraid. I buried it instead of filing it. I'm sorry."*

### Entry Hall

#### Shadow
[D&D Beyond](https://www.dndbeyond.com/monsters/17006-shadow) | CR 1/2 | Medium Undead | Chaotic Evil

| AC | HP | Speed |
|---|---|---|
| 12 | 16 (3d8+3) | 40 ft. |

**Ability Scores:** STR 6 (−2), DEX 14 (+2), CON 13 (+1), INT 6 (−2), WIS 10 (+0), CHA 8 (−1)  
**Skills:** Stealth +4 (Stealth +6 in dim light or darkness)  
**Vulnerabilities:** Radiant  
**Immunities:** Necrotic, Poison | **Condition:** Exhaustion, Frightened, Grappled, Paralyzed, Petrified, Poisoned, Prone, Restrained  
**Senses:** Darkvision 60 ft.

**Traits:**
- **Amorphous.** Can move through a space as narrow as 1 inch wide.
- **Shadow Stealth.** Bonus action Hide in dim light or darkness.
- **Sunlight Weakness.** Disadvantage on attack rolls and ability checks in direct sunlight.

**Actions:**
- **Strength Drain.** +4 to hit, 5 ft.; Hit 9 (2d6+2) necrotic damage; target's STR score reduced by 1d4. Dies if STR reaches 0. Reduction recovers after long rest. A humanoid killed this way rises as a Shadow 1d4 hours later.

> **Campaign Role:** Three Shadows inhabit the darkened entry hall of the Sunken Hacienda — residual grief given incorporeal form, not commanded creatures. A DC 13 Religion check identifies that *Sacred Flame* or *Turn Undead* will disperse them without requiring a full combat. Their STR Drain mechanic is punishing if ignored; the party should feel incentivized to use the Religion option once identified. Station them in the corners of the hall, barely visible until the party moves toward the staircase.

**Resistances:** Acid, Cold, Fire, Lightning, Thunder; B/P/S from nonmagical, non-silvered weapons

**Traits:**
- **Incorporeal Movement.** As above.

**Actions:**
- **Life Drain.** +6 to hit, 5 ft.; Hit 21 (4d8+3) necrotic damage. DC 14 CON or HP max reduced by same amount until long rest. Target dies if max HP reaches 0.
- **Create Specter (1/Day).** Targets a humanoid killed within the last minute. That humanoid rises as a Specter under the Wraith's control.

> **Campaign Role:** The Wraith in the children's room is the fused spiritual presence of Esperanza's two children — not hostile by default. It registers as communicating grief rather than rage. A party that attempts to speak with it (DC 16 Persuasion or DC 14 Insight) hears it communicate in fragments: *mother*, *cold*, *she didn't mean it*, *she was already gone when she did it*. A party that speaks with the Wraith rather than fighting it gains **advantage on all saving throws against La Llorona's Mourning Wail** in Act 5. If the party attacks immediately without attempt at contact, the Wraith fights normally. Consider having it reach toward the party member who has expressed the most empathy before initiation — giving the party a moment to reconsider.

|---|---|---|
| 13 | 136 (16d8+64) | 30 ft. |

**Ability Scores:** STR 18 (+4), DEX 14 (+2), CON 18 (+4), INT 13 (+1), WIS 16 (+3), CHA 18 (+4)  
**Saves:** STR +9, CON +9, WIS +8, CHA +9  
**Skills:** Athletics +12, Intimidation +9, Perception +8  
**Resistances:** B/P/S from nonmagical weapons  
**Immunities:** Poison | **Condition:** Charmed, Exhaustion, Frightened, Paralyzed, Poisoned, Stunned  
**Senses:** Darkvision 60 ft., Truesight 20 ft.

**Traits:**
- **Regeneration.** Regains 10 HP at the start of each turn. If reduced to 0 HP, it drops but rises again in 1 minute with 136 HP unless destroyed by *divine intervention* or has achieved its goal.
- **Rejuvenation.** If the Revenant's goal is not satisfied and it is destroyed, it reconstitutes in its body within 24 hours. Only satisfied (goal achieved) or kept from its target for 1 year and 1 day can end it permanently.
- **Turn Immunity.** Cannot be turned by *Turn Undead*.

**Actions (Multiattack — 2 Fist attacks):**
- **Fist.** +9 to hit, 5 ft.; Hit 14 (3d6+4) bludgeoning damage. Target: DC 17 STR or grappled.
- **Haunt (Recharge 6).** DC 17 WIS save or frightened and paralyzed for 1 minute. Repeat save at end of each turn to end.

**Reactions:**
- **Protective Reaction.** When a creature within 5 ft. is attacked, grants the creature a +4 bonus to AC.

> **Campaign Role:** The Revenant in the flooded basement is **Mateo Reyes** — a spiritual echo of the man who died trying to expose the Alcalde, trapped in the basement of the house where his family was destroyed. He is not hostile to those who carry his evidence. Presenting Mateo's ledger or letter causes the Revenant to stand down — it bows, returns to the water, and does not reconstitute while the party is pursuing justice. If the party fights without presenting evidence, it cannot be permanently destroyed here — it will reconstitute within 24 hours until Vásquez confesses or dies. The Regeneration and Rejuvenation traits reinforce the fiction: Mateo cannot rest until the truth is told.

#### La Llorona (Esperanza Vargas de Reyes)
Custom Boss | CR 13 | Medium Undead | Chaotic Neutral
*Base: Banshee (heavily modified for levels 13–15)*
[D&D Beyond Banshee Reference](https://www.dndbeyond.com/monsters/16804-banshee)

| AC | HP | Speed |
|---|---|---|
| 13 | 168 (16d8+96) | 40 ft., Fly 40 ft. (hover), Swim 40 ft. |

**Ability Scores:** STR 1 (−5), DEX 14 (+2), CON 22 (+6), INT 12 (+1), WIS 16 (+3), CHA 20 (+5)  
**Saves:** WIS +8, CHA +10  
**Skills:** Perception +8  
**Damage Resistances:** Acid, Fire, Lightning, Thunder; Bludgeoning, Piercing, and Slashing from nonmagical attacks  
**Damage Immunities:** Cold, Necrotic, Poison  
**Condition Immunities:** Charmed, Exhaustion, Frightened, Grappled, Paralyzed, Petrified, Poisoned, Prone, Restrained  
**Senses:** Darkvision 120 ft., Truesight 30 ft.  
**Languages:** Understands all languages she knew in life; speaks only in weeping and fragments

**Traits:**

- **Incorporeal Movement.** La Llorona can move through creatures and objects as if they were difficult terrain. She takes 1d10 force damage if she ends her turn inside an object.

- **Lament Aura.** Each creature that starts its turn within 30 feet of La Llorona must succeed on a DC 17 Wisdom saving throw or be pulled 30 feet directly toward the river and have its speed reduced by 10 feet until the start of its next turn. A creature that succeeds is immune to Lament Aura for 24 hours.

- **Deathbound.** La Llorona cannot be reduced below 1 hit point unless the truth of Mateo's death has been spoken aloud by a living witness (Rite of Naming, Act 4). Without this condition met, any damage that would reduce her to 0 HP instead reduces her to 1 HP.

- **River Empowerment.** While within 60 feet of the Río de las Sombras, La Llorona regains 10 HP at the start of each of her turns.

**Actions (Multiattack — 2 × Grieving Touch):**

- **Grieving Touch.** *Melee Spell Attack:* +10 to hit, reach 5 ft. Hit: 19 (4d6+5) necrotic damage. DC 17 Constitution save or the target's HP maximum is reduced by the same amount until the target finishes a long rest. A humanoid slain by this attack rises as a Specter under La Llorona's control at the start of her next turn.

- **Mourning Wail (Recharge 5–6).** La Llorona emits a keening shriek. All living creatures within 60 feet that can hear it must succeed on a DC 18 Constitution saving throw. On failure: drop to 0 hit points. On success: take 44 (8d10) psychic damage. *If the Rite of Naming was completed in Act 4, the save DC is reduced to 14 and maximum damage is halved.*

- **Drown the Living (3/Day).** La Llorona targets one creature within 30 feet. The target must succeed on a DC 17 Strength saving throw or be dragged up to 30 feet toward the river and restrained in phantasmal water. The target is considered restrained and begins suffocating (PHB drowning rules) until it breaks free (DC 17 Strength, repeated each turn as a bonus action) or La Llorona is incapacitated.

**Reactions:**

- **Deflect Grief.** When La Llorona takes Radiant damage, she may use her reaction to halve the damage and reflect 9 (2d8) psychic damage back at the attacker (no attack roll required).

**Legendary Actions (3/round, at end of another creature's turn):**

- **Weep.** All creatures within 30 feet take 9 (2d8) psychic damage.
- **Glide.** La Llorona moves up to her fly speed without provoking opportunity attacks.
- **Summon the Drowned (Costs 2).** La Llorona calls a Zombie (CR 1/4) or Ghoul (CR 1) from the river. Up to 4 summoned creatures can be active at one time.

**Resolution Mechanics:**

| Condition | Effect |
|---|---|
| Rite of Naming completed (Act 4) | Mourning Wail DC reduced to 14; maximum damage halved |
| Wraith in Children's Room spoken to (Act 3) | Party gains advantage on all CON saves vs. Mourning Wail |
| Mateo's letter read aloud as an action during combat | Deathbound trait drops; River Empowerment drops; Mourning Wail unusable for remainder of combat |
| All three conditions met | La Llorona can be defeated and laid to rest permanently |
| Conditions not met | La Llorona reduced to 1 HP only; *Banishment* (DC 17) pushes her away for 1 minute |

> **Campaign Role:** La Llorona is the campaign's final boss and its moral center simultaneously. She is not a monster who attacks the town because she is evil — she is a woman who died waiting for justice and has been trapped in grief so long that the grief has consumed her. Her Deathbound trait means a party that ignores the investigation and comes straight to fight her will hit a wall — the answer is never more damage, it is always the truth. The Mourning Wail is her most lethal ability at this level; a level 13 party should feel genuine fear of it. The peaceful resolution is harder to achieve but is the mechanically superior outcome — completing the Act 4 Rite and recovering Mateo's letter rewards preparation.
