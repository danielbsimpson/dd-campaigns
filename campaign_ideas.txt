Campaign Ideas
Based on mythology from each continent
North America
1. The Wendigo (Algonquian Mythology)
   * Story: The Wendigo is a malevolent spirit associated with cannibalism, winter, and greed. It’s said to possess humans, turning them into monstrous creatures driven by an insatiable hunger.
   * Campaign Idea: The players are tasked with investigating a series of gruesome murders in a remote, snow-covered village. They soon discover that a powerful Wendigo has been awakened, and they must track it down and stop it before it consumes the entire community.
2. The Thunderbird (Native American Mythology)
   * Story: The Thunderbird is a giant bird said to bring thunder, lightning, and storms. It’s a powerful, sometimes wrathful, protector of humans against evil spirits.
   * Campaign Idea: A sacred artifact has been stolen, and the Thunderbird is unleashing devastating storms in its rage. The players must retrieve the artifact and return it to its rightful place to calm the Thunderbird before it destroys the region.
3. The Legend of La Llorona (Mexican Folklore)
   * Story: La Llorona, or "The Weeping Woman," is the ghost of a woman who drowned her children in a fit of madness and now wanders near bodies of water, crying and looking for them.
   * Campaign Idea: Children are going missing near a cursed river, and the players must investigate. They soon learn that La Llorona is behind the disappearances and must find a way to lay her spirit to rest, possibly by uncovering the true story behind her tragic actions.
4. The Aztec Underworld (Mictlan)
   * Story: Mictlan is the Aztec underworld, a nine-level realm where souls of the dead journey through trials before reaching eternal rest.
   * Campaign Idea: The players must venture into Mictlan to retrieve a stolen soul or to seek the wisdom of an ancient spirit. They must navigate the nine levels, each with its own challenges and guardians, before they can achieve their goal.
5. The Deer Woman (Native American Mythology)
   * Story: The Deer Woman is a spirit associated with fertility, love, and the wilderness, but she can also be dangerous, leading men to their doom if they disrespect her or the natural world.
   * Campaign Idea: A village suffers from a series of strange deaths, with men found trampled and gored. The players must investigate and discover that the Deer Woman is punishing those who have desecrated a sacred forest. They must find a way to appease her before more lives are lost.
South America
1. El Dorado (Colombian Mythology)
   * Story: El Dorado, the legendary city of gold, was believed to be a place of immense wealth hidden deep in the Amazon jungle.
   * Campaign Idea: The players are hired by a wealthy patron to find the lost city of El Dorado. Along the way, they face hostile tribes, cursed jungles, and rival treasure hunters, only to discover that the city holds a dark secret that could spell doom for those who seek it.
2. The Chullachaqui (Peruvian Folklore)
   * Story: The Chullachaqui is a forest spirit that can shape-shift to lure people into the jungle, where they become lost forever.
   * Campaign Idea: Villagers have gone missing in the jungle, and the players are asked to find them. They discover that the Chullachaqui is leading people astray. To stop it, they must uncover the spirit’s true identity and intentions, possibly facing it in a maze-like, ever-changing forest.
3. The Legend of the Moai (Easter Island)
   * Story: The Moai are massive stone statues created by the Rapa Nui people of Easter Island. Legends say they were moved by supernatural means, possibly through the help of ancestral spirits.
   * Campaign Idea: The Moai have begun to move again, causing earthquakes and other disasters. The players must discover why these ancient statues have come to life and how to stop them, possibly dealing with angry spirits or uncovering ancient magic that once controlled them.
4. The Curse of the Potosi Mine (Bolivian Folklore)
   * Story: The Potosi mine was once the richest silver mine in the world, but it was also the site of immense suffering and death. It’s said to be cursed by the spirits of those who died there.
   * Campaign Idea: Miners in the region have started to vanish, and strange phenomena are occurring around the old Potosi mine. The players must delve into the cursed mine, facing the wrathful spirits of those who perished there, to end the curse and bring peace to the area.
5. The Yacumama (Amazonian Mythology)
   * Story: The Yacumama is a giant serpent said to inhabit the Amazon River. It’s believed to be the mother of all water creatures and can swallow boats and people whole.
   * Campaign Idea: A river village is under attack by the Yacumama, and the players are tasked with stopping it. They must journey deep into the Amazon, facing other monstrous creatures and hostile environments, to find and defeat the Yacumama or find a way to appease it.
Europe
1. The Wild Hunt (Germanic and Celtic Mythology)
   * Story: The Wild Hunt is a ghostly procession of hunters, often led by a god or spirit, that roams the night sky. Encountering the Hunt is a bad omen, often foretelling war or disaster.
   * Campaign Idea: The players are caught in a small village plagued by nightly visits from the Wild Hunt. They must find a way to either banish the Hunt or survive its deadly toll, uncovering ancient rituals or battling the spectral riders themselves.
2. Baba Yaga (Slavic Folklore)
   * Story: Baba Yaga is a fearsome witch who lives in a hut that stands on chicken legs. She’s known for her unpredictability, sometimes helping and sometimes hindering those who seek her out.
   * Campaign Idea: The players need the assistance of Baba Yaga, but first, they must find her moving hut in the deep, enchanted forest. Once they do, they must either bargain with her or complete a series of dangerous tasks she sets for them, all while navigating her ever-changing, treacherous domain.
3. The Tuatha Dé Danann (Irish Mythology)
   * Story: The Tuatha Dé Danann are a race of god-like beings who inhabited Ireland before the arrival of the Celts. They are skilled in magic and are often associated with the fae.
   * Campaign Idea: The players are drawn into a conflict between humans and the Tuatha Dé Danann, who seek to reclaim the land they once ruled. They must navigate both the human and fae courts, dealing with powerful magic and ancient grudges, possibly leading to a climactic battle between the two sides.
4. The Greek Underworld (Greek Mythology)
   * Story: The Greek Underworld, ruled by Hades, is where souls go after death. It’s a realm of shadows, rivers, and various deities and monsters.
   * Campaign Idea: The players must journey to the Underworld to retrieve a lost soul or artifact. They’ll need to navigate the treacherous rivers, avoid the wrath of Hades, and outwit or battle the various guardians and monsters that dwell there.
5. The Pied Piper of Hamelin (German Folklore)
   * Story: The Pied Piper was hired to rid the town of Hamelin of rats, which he did by playing a magical pipe. When the townspeople refused to pay, he used the pipe to lead their children away.
   * Campaign Idea: A town is plagued by a mysterious figure playing a magical tune, leading away both the town’s children and its wealth. The players must find and confront the Piper, discovering whether they can redeem him, break his curse, or stop him by force.
Africa
1. Anansi the Spider (West African Mythology)
   * Story: Anansi is a trickster god who often uses his cunning and wit to overcome challenges or trick others, including other gods and animals.
   * Campaign Idea: The players encounter Anansi, who offers to help them achieve a difficult goal—if they can solve his riddles or complete tasks that involve trickery and deception. However, they must be wary of being outsmarted by Anansi himself.
2. The Great Zimbabwe (Shona Mythology)
   * Story: The ruins of Great Zimbabwe are often associated with legends of a great kingdom and powerful magic. It’s said that the city was protected by spirits and had access to vast riches.
   * Campaign Idea: The players are hired to explore the ruins of Great Zimbabwe, rumored to be the site of a lost treasure or ancient power. They must navigate traps, hostile spirits, and possibly rival adventurers seeking the same prize, all while uncovering the history and fate of the once-great city.
3. The Mokele-Mbembe (Congolese Folklore)
   * Story: The Mokele-Mbembe is a legendary creature said to inhabit the Congo River basin, often described as a living dinosaur.
   * Campaign Idea: A wealthy patron hires the players to capture or study the Mokele-Mbembe, but the journey into the Congo reveals more dangers than they anticipated, including hostile tribes, deadly wildlife, and other mythic creatures that guard the deep jungle.
4. The Myth of Sundiata (Mali Empire Mythology)
   * Story: Sundiata Keita is the legendary founder of the Mali Empire, known for his strength, wisdom, and overcoming adversity.
   * Campaign Idea: The players are involved in the rise of a new empire, either as allies or adversaries to a figure reminiscent of Sundiata. They must navigate political intrigue, battles, and the influence of powerful spirits or gods who have a stake in the empire’s success or failure.
5. The Zulu Heavens (Zulu Mythology)
   * Story: In Zulu mythology, the heavens are ruled by the god uNkulunkulu, and are a place of powerful deities and ancestral spirits.
   * Campaign Idea: The players are chosen by a Zulu shaman to undertake a spiritual journey to the heavens to seek the favor of the gods or the advice of ancestors. Along the way, they face trials, otherworldly creatures, and must prove their worthiness to complete their quest.
Asia
1. The Monkey King (Chinese Mythology)
   * Story: The Monkey King, Sun Wukong, is a trickster and warrior with immense strength and magical abilities. He’s known for his rebellious nature and journey to the West.
   * Campaign Idea: The players join or face off against a powerful figure like the Monkey King in a quest that involves retrieving sacred texts or defeating powerful demons. They must deal with his cunning tricks and immense power while completing their mission.
2. The Tale of the Heike (Japanese Folklore)
   * Story: The Tale of the Heike is an epic recounting the fall of the Taira clan in Japan, featuring themes of honor, revenge, and the impermanence of life.
   * Campaign Idea: The players are involved in a samurai war, choosing sides between rival clans. They must navigate the complex code of honor, engage in battles, and deal with the consequences of their actions, possibly facing vengeful spirits or curses.
3. The Rakshasa (Hindu Mythology)
   * Story: Rakshasas are powerful and malevolent demons in Hindu mythology, known for their shape-shifting abilities and magic.
   * Campaign Idea: The players must track down and defeat a Rakshasa that is terrorizing a region, using its shape-shifting abilities to sow chaos. The campaign could involve uncovering the Rakshasa’s true identity and battling through its illusions and traps.
4. The Mahabharata (Indian Epic)
   * Story: The Mahabharata is one of the great Indian epics, telling the story of the Kurukshetra War and the fates of the Kaurava and Pandava princes.
   * Campaign Idea: The players are involved in a massive war, reminiscent of the Kurukshetra War, where they must choose sides, deal with divine intervention, and navigate the complexities of dharma (duty) and karma. The campaign could culminate in an epic battle that determines the fate of a kingdom.
5. The Naga (Southeast Asian Mythology)
   * Story: Naga are serpent-like beings in Southeast Asian mythology, often seen as protectors of water and wealth but can also be vengeful.
   * Campaign Idea: The players must seek the favor of the Naga to protect a region from drought or to gain access to hidden treasures. However, they must be careful not to offend these powerful beings, which could lead to devastating floods or other natural disasters.
Australia and Oceania
1. The Rainbow Serpent (Aboriginal Australian Mythology)
   * Story: The Rainbow Serpent is a creator god and a symbol of fertility, often associated with water and the creation of the landscape.
   * Campaign Idea: The players must appease the Rainbow Serpent to end a drought or restore balance to the land. This might involve a journey to sacred sites, performing ancient rituals, and battling those who would disrupt the natural order.
2. Maui’s Fishing Adventure (Polynesian Mythology)
   * Story: Maui is a trickster hero in Polynesian mythology who, among other feats, fished up entire islands from the ocean.
   * Campaign Idea: The players join a demigod on a quest to retrieve a powerful artifact or raise a new landmass. Along the way, they must deal with sea monsters, treacherous waters, and rival deities or spirits who seek to stop them.
3. The Legend of the Taniwha (Maori Mythology)
   * Story: Taniwha are powerful, dragon-like creatures in Maori mythology, often guardians of certain areas but also capable of destruction.
   * Campaign Idea: A Taniwha has become enraged and is attacking settlements. The players must either pacify or defeat the creature, which may involve uncovering the reason for its anger, possibly a desecration of its sacred space, or dealing with those who seek to exploit its power.
4. The Legend of Pele (Hawaiian Mythology)
   * Story: Pele is the Hawaiian goddess of volcanoes, known for her passionate and fiery nature, both creating and destroying with her lava.
   * Campaign Idea: The players must stop an imminent volcanic eruption by appeasing or negotiating with Pele. This might involve a dangerous journey to the volcano’s heart, dealing with elemental creatures, and performing a ritual to calm the goddess’s anger.
5. The Dreamtime (Aboriginal Australian Mythology)
   * Story: Dreamtime is a foundational concept in Aboriginal Australian mythology, referring to the time when ancestral spirits created the world and laid down laws for living.
   * Campaign Idea: The players enter the Dreamtime to solve a great mystery or to seek the wisdom of the Ancestors. The Dreamtime is a surreal and magical place, where the landscape can change at a moment’s notice, and the players’ actions can affect both the spiritual and physical worlds.
