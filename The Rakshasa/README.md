# The Rakshasa

A D&D 5e campaign set in a South Asian-inspired kingdom, designed for parties of levels 12–14. A shapeshifting demon has spent decades hollowing out a prosperous city from within — and the adventurers are the only ones who can stop it.

---

## Overview

An exhausted envoy arrives seeking outside help. The great city of **Vethara** is dying from within — nobles behave strangely, trusted allies betray their friends, and the ruling council grows more erratic with each passing moon. Behind it all, the demon **Vikramasura** — an ancient Rakshasa of terrible cunning — has quietly replaced Vethara's most powerful figures one by one, building toward a single terrible ritual that will cement its dominion over the region forever.

**Tone:** Political intrigue, social deception, investigation, mounting horror  
**Setting:** The walled city of Vethara and the surrounding jungle  
**Themes:** Identity and trust, illusion versus reality, the corrupting nature of power, divine intervention

---

## The City of Vethara

Vethara is a wealthy, walled city-state built along the banks of the sacred **Mala River**, famed for its spice trade, towering temples, and the richest bazaar in the known world. Its architecture is a marvel of carved stone and gilded towers, with the **Palace of a Thousand Lamps** looming over everything from its hilltop perch.

From the outside, Vethara appears prosperous and magnificent. But something is wrong. Merchants vanish. Priests are found dead at their altars. Children disappear from the lower wards without a sign of struggle. The city guard arrests the wrong people. The Maharaja speaks but says nothing useful. Those who have known Vethara's leaders for decades whisper that they no longer recognize them.

### Key NPCs

- **Maharaja Vardhan** — The well-loved ruler of Vethara; increasingly isolated and distant; may already have been replaced by the time the party arrives
- **Grand Vizier Kiran** — The Maharaja's most trusted advisor and the Rakshasa's primary disguise; players should not learn this immediately
- **Captain Priya Deva** — Head of the city guard; suspicious of recent events but outmatched; the party's primary ally throughout the campaign
- **Yogi Ananda** — An ancient and eccentric sage living in retirement in the Scholar's Quarter; one of the very few people in Vethara who understands what a Rakshasa truly is
- **Nalini** — A spice merchant whose husband vanished weeks ago; the party's first quest giver in the city
- **Chief Priest Arjuna** — High priest of the Temple of Indra; under direct, escalating attack from the Rakshasa's cult network
- **Siva** — A thief and fence operating out of the Night Bazaar; the party's underworld contact and key information broker
- **General Abhaya** — Commander of Vethara's army; replaced by a Nycaloth; a disaster waiting to happen at the coronation ceremony

## Campaign Structure

### Act 0 — The Call

An envoy — exhausted, traveling light, with no military escort — arrives at the party's current location bearing a sealed letter from Captain Priya Deva. The letter is brief:

> *"Vethara is not well. Its sickness comes from within and wears familiar faces. I cannot trust anyone already inside these walls. Come quickly, and come quietly."*

The party is offered a substantial advance and the promise of further payment upon resolving Vethara's troubles. They are asked to enter the city as ordinary travelers — not as champions. The Rakshasa must not know it is being hunted.

---

### Act 1 — The City of Vethara

**Arrival:** The party enters Vethara through the River Gate. The city is magnificent — but the mood is uneasy. Guards are jumpy. Temples are half-staffed. Merchants speak in lowered voices and do not meet strangers' eyes.

**Meeting Priya Deva:** Captain Priya meets the party privately at the Crimson Lotus Inn after dark. She lays out what she knows: half a dozen nobles and city officials have visibly "changed" over the past two years. Her investigation has been stonewalled at every turn by the Grand Vizier. Three priests of Indra were murdered last month. Children in the Sunken Ward have been disappearing for six weeks. She believes the events are connected but cannot prove it.

**Investigation Phase:** Much of Act 1 is social and investigative. The party must build trust with the city's residents, gather information, and decide which threads to pursue first. Priya's dossier flags four active situations — each can be explored as a side quest, pursued in parallel, or combined into a single investigation across the city.

#### Side Quests

**1. The Merchant's Thread**

Nalini, a spice merchant at the Spice Exchange, hires the party to find her missing husband Rajan, who vanished after arranging a late-night meeting with a new buyer for a large shipment. Investigation through the Grand Bazaar and into the Night Bazaar leads to a **Cambion-run smuggling ring** operating out of Vethara's warehouse district. Rajan is alive but being held as leverage — the smugglers are moving ritual components for the Rakshasa's network.

Steps to Complete:
- Question Nalini for details on the buyer, the shipment, and the meeting location
- Gather information at the Night Bazaar; roll for contact with Siva
- Track the smuggling operation to the warehouse district
- Infiltrate the warehouse and defeat the Cambion's operation
- Free Rajan and recover evidence linking the smugglers to the palace

- *Enemies:* Cambion (CR 5), Shadow Demons (CR 4), Bearded Devils (CR 3)
- *Reward:* 500 gold from Nalini + *Necklace of Fireballs*, Siva becomes an active informant, partial map of the cult's underground supply routes

---

**2. The Temple of Indra**

Chief Priest Arjuna asks the party to investigate the murders of three of his temple brothers. Each was found drained of life at their personal shrine, with their offerings consumed and sacred texts missing. The trail leads beneath a crumbling district of the city to a desecrated sub-temple where a **Night Hag coven** has made its nest. The Hags are harvesting life-essence and souls for the Rakshasa's preparation ritual.

Steps to Complete:
- Examine the murder scenes for clues; Religion or Investigation check (DC 14) identifies the soul-draining signs as hag-work
- Question temple records to identify the stolen texts — they are pages of a summoning codex
- Locate the sub-temple entrance (Siva can help; or follow tracks from the murder scenes)
- Navigate the sub-temple's hazards and defeat the coven
- Recover the stolen codex pages and bring them to Yogi Ananda for analysis

- *Enemies:* Night Hag (CR 5) × 3, Shadow Demons (CR 4)
- *Reward:* Blessings of Indra (one use of *Death Ward* per character), *Eye of the Hag* *(see Key Rewards)*, the stolen codex pages reveal the ritual's final phase when analyzed by Yogi Ananda

---

**3. The Missing Children**

Eleven children have vanished from the Sunken Ward over six weeks with no signs of struggle. Families are desperate and the city guard has done nothing. Siva (if recruited from the Merchant's Thread) points the party toward a storm drain entrance that has seen unusual activity at night. An **Oni** in the guise of a kindly city welfare inspector has been systematically abducting children and holding them in a hidden underground facility — insurance to prevent the Sunken Ward's community leaders from causing trouble.

Steps to Complete:
- Speak with families in the Sunken Ward to establish a pattern (all children were approached by the welfare inspector)
- Locate and enter the storm drain network
- Navigate the underground holding facility, bypassing Chain Devil guards
- Defeat the Oni (it will attempt to maintain its disguise until cornered) and free the children
- Return the children safely and speak with the Sunken Ward community leaders

- *Enemies:* Oni (CR 7) — the false inspector, Chain Devil (CR 8) × 2 guarding the prison
- *Reward:* Gratitude and intelligence network of the Sunken Ward community, *Amulet of the Devout* *(see Key Rewards)*

---

**4. The False General**

Captain Priya has noticed that General Abhaya, commander of Vethara's entire army, has been issuing unusual orders — rotating proven officers out of sensitive posts and replacing them with unfamiliar faces. His demeanor has also changed: cold, precise, and disinterested in people he once treated as old friends. A stakeout and investigation reveals the general studying non-human materials in private. Investigation (or a Well-timed *True Seeing* or *Detect Evil and Good*) confirms what the evidence suggests: **a Nycaloth has replaced the general**. If exposed before the Maharaja's upcoming coronation ceremony, the party prevents a staged assassination attempt designed to consolidate the Rakshasa's control of the city in a single night.

Steps to Complete:
- Investigate Priya's suspicions by speaking with officers who have been transferred
- Conduct surveillance on the general's private chambers — find non-human signs
- Plan and execute the exposure: confront the Nycaloth with evidence sufficient to force the issue
- Defeat the Nycaloth and its devil guards before they can send word to Vikramasura
- Free the real General Abhaya from his hidden prison beneath the barracks

- *Enemies:* Nycaloth (CR 9) posing as General Abhaya, Bearded Devil (CR 3) × 4 as "trusted guard"
- *Reward:* General Abhaya's military ring (grants command of city guard resources), *Ring of Mind Shielding* *(see Key Rewards)*

---

### Act 2 — The Shadow Network

With enough threads pulled from Act 1, the picture sharpens: a coordinated, patient operation has been placing disguised fiends in positions of power throughout Vethara. No single informant sees the whole shape of it — but together, the smuggling routes, the ritual components, the stolen codex, the underground prison, and the false general's troop rotations all connect to one source. The supply chain originates from a sealed administrative wing of the Palace.

**Connecting the Dots:** The party must compile their evidence and present it to Captain Priya. When she sees it laid out, she acknowledges that the trail leads directly to the Grand Vizier's office and that she cannot pursue it through official channels without the Vizier knowing.

**The Night Bazaar:** To access the palace supply chain without triggering an alert, the party must work through Siva and the Night Bazaar's network. Siva will commit only if the party has demonstrated genuine goodwill — completing at least one of the Act 1 side quests in a way that protected rather than exploited the city's people.

**The Bone Devil's Vault:** The supply chain terminates at a fortified underground vault beneath the merchant district, accessible through a concealed entrance in the Night Bazaar. The vault is guarded by two **Bone Devils** and a squad of cult fanatics. Inside: ritual components, ledgers naming every replaced official in Vethara, and Infernal communications in the Grand Vizier's own handwriting.

- *Enemies:* Bone Devil (CR 9) × 2, Cult Fanatics (CR 2)
- *Reward:* Full dossier on the Rakshasa's network; irrefutable evidence linking Vikramasura to the Grand Vizier's identity

---

### Act 3 — The Palace of Whispers

The vault's evidence is enough to bring down the Grand Vizier — if the party can get it in front of Maharaja Vardhan. Vikramasura controls every formal channel of access to the Maharaja and will work quickly to discredit, detain, or eliminate the party before an audience can be arranged.

**Getting the Audience:** The party must find a way past the Vizier's gatekeeping. Options include:

| Approach | Method | Risk |
|---|---|---|
| Formal channels | Go through Priya's official request process | Slow; the Vizier will be alerted |
| Military escort | Use General Abhaya's ring for direct access | Fast if Act 1 Quest 4 was completed |
| Palace contacts | Siva's network has a sympathetic chamberlain | Requires bribing or charming the contact |
| Infiltration | Enter the palace grounds without an invitation | Stealth-based; guards are on high alert |

**The Vipers at Court:** Inside the palace, the Rakshasa has placed agents among the courtiers. The party must navigate a social gauntlet of deceived, replaced, and genuine nobles to identify who can still be trusted and who is already reporting to Vikramasura.

**Unmasking the Vizier:** Once the party presents their evidence, the Maharaja summons Grand Vizier Kiran. Vikramasura's disguise is deep and confident — it will attempt to paint the party as foreign provocateurs and their evidence as fabricated. However, Yogi Ananda (if consulted previously) has provided the party with a crucial tool: a **consecrated incense** whose smoke dispels magical disguises and illusions within its area.

**The Vizier Revealed:** When the incense smoke reaches the Vizier, the disguise collapses. Vikramasura's true form — a tiger-headed demon with hands fitted on backwards — materializes in the council chamber. It does not panic. It taunts the party. The ritual is nearly complete. They are already too late. Then it tears open a dimensional rift in the palace wall and vanishes into the jungle.

- *Boss Encounter (escape fight):* Vikramasura (Rakshasa, CR 13) — not designed as a kill fight; the goal is to survive and note the direction of its escape
- *Enemies:* Erinyes (CR 12) × 2 — Vikramasura's personal guards; fight to the death to cover the escape

---

### Act 4 — The Jungle Road

**The Chase:** Vikramasura has fled to its hidden sanctum — an ancient ruined temple complex deep in the surrounding jungle, used as the staging ground for the great summoning ritual. The party must move immediately: the ritual can be completed within days.

**Jungle Hazards:** The approaches to the temple are watched, warded, and actively patrolled by the Rakshasa's remaining outer network.

| Encounter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Vine Devil Snare** | Magical alarm wards strung between the trees; trigger a Chain Devil ambush if the party moves carelessly |
| **The False Path** | An illusory fork in the road diverts travelers onto the wrong track; the false path ends in a Shadow Demon ambush |
| **The Oni Guardian** | A second Oni patrols the jungle perimeter, disguised as a local hermit gathering herbs near the road |
| **The Yuan-ti Outpost** | A band of Yuan-ti cultists hired by the Rakshasa man a watchtower at the jungle's edge; they can be avoided, talked past, or fought |

**The Guardian Naga:** At the base of the temple ruins, the party encounters a **Guardian Naga** — not an enemy. The Naga has been magically bound to this gate for decades against its will, forced to serve as a deterrent. It is exhausted, bitter, and deeply resentful of its captor. If the party speaks with it and offers to break the binding once Vikramasura is defeated, the Naga will stand aside and share what it knows about the temple's layout — including where the ritual circles are and the fastest route to the inner sanctum.

- *Enemies:* Chain Devil (CR 8), Shadow Demon (CR 4) × 2, Oni (CR 7), Yuan-ti Abomination (CR 7)
- *Conditional Ally:* Guardian Naga (CR 10) — stands aside and provides temple intelligence if the party promises to free it post-battle

---

### Act 5 — The Temple of Vikramasura

An ancient temple reclaimed and expanded by Vikramasura over decades of occupation. Three ascending tiers of chambers lead to the inner sanctum where the summoning ritual is being finalized. The party must fight upward through each tier — and destroying the ritual circles as they go will directly weaken the final confrontation.

---

#### Tier 1 — The Hall of False Faces

The entry hall is a gallery of the Rakshasa's past victories — walls lined with the lacquered faces of every person Vikramasura has ever impersonated, each one detailed and labeled with a name and a date. The chamber is designed to disorient: mirrors line the side passages, creating an overlapping maze of reflections, and illusion magic fills dead-end corridors with convincing copies of real rooms.

- *Enemies:* Shadow Demons (CR 4) × 3, Cambion (CR 5) × 2
- *Hazard:* Perception DC 14 or Investigation DC 16 to navigate correctly; failure leads into an ambush chamber

---

#### Tier 2 — The Ritual Chambers

The mid-level of the temple contains eleven carved stone ritual circles — each holding a soul harvested by the Night Hag coven. Sickly light pulses from each circle, and the air thrums with the near-complete summoning. Destroying a circle (AC 12, 20 HP, immune to piercing and psychic) releases the trapped soul and weakens the ritual.

> **Momentum Rule:** Each ritual circle destroyed here reduces Vikramasura's maximum HP by 10 in the final fight. If 6 or more circles are destroyed, the Efreeti enters Phase 2 at half its normal maximum HP.

- *Enemies:* Bone Devil (CR 9) × 2, Erinyes (CR 12) — stationed to protect the circles; the Erinyes will prioritize stopping the party from destroying circles over dealing damage
- *Objective:* Destroy as many circles as possible before advancing to Tier 3

---

#### Tier 3 — The Inner Sanctum

The throne room of the temple. Vikramasura stands at the center of a massive summoning circle, the Efreeti half-materialized in a tower of flame above the altar. For the first time in the campaign, the Rakshasa is fully itself — no disguise, no pretense, no patience for social games.

**The Battle — Two Phases:**

**Phase 1:** The party battles Vikramasura while the last elements of the ritual run. Temple acolytes maintain the summoning — killing them slows the completion. Destroying any remaining circles (if the party did not complete them in Tier 2) also disrupts the ritual.

**Phase 2:** Once the timer expires or all acolytes fall, the **Efreeti** fully materializes and enters the fight alongside Vikramasura. If 6+ circles were destroyed in Tier 2, the Efreeti enters at half HP.

| Creature | CR | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Vikramasura (Rakshasa) | 13 | Immunity to spells of 6th level or lower; innate spellcasting; regenerates 10 HP per turn if any ritual circles remain intact |
| Efreeti | 11 | Enters at full HP if fewer than 6 circles were destroyed; half HP otherwise |
| Temple Acolytes | — | Cultists maintaining the final summoning elements; eliminating them triggers Phase 2 early |

- *Reward:* Vikramasura's Mantle *(see Key Rewards)*, the Efreeti's bound lamp *(see Key Rewards)*

---

### Act 6 — Resolution

With Vikramasura destroyed, the remaining fiends scattered across Vethara lose their patron and begin to unravel — some flee, some turn on each other, and the last few are quickly rooted out by Captain Priya and the restored General Abhaya. The replaced nobles and officials are freed where they still live; where they did not survive, the truth is revealed and mourned.

Maharaja Vardhan hosts a grand ceremony at the Temple of Indra, presenting the party with formal titles, land grants, and estates within Vethara. Chief Priest Arjuna consecrates the party in the name of the gods. The city's people fill the streets.

On the eve of the celebration, Yogi Ananda quietly pulls the party aside. He is pleased — but not at ease.

> *"Vikramasura was powerful. But it was not the most powerful of its kind. It belongs to a lineage. Others of that lineage will notice what happened here. They will know that outsiders destroyed one of their own in Vethara. They will want to know who."*

The city of Vethara is safe. The world beyond Vethara is now watching.
