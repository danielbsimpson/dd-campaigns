### Quest 1: The Merchant's Thread

#### Bearded Devil
[D&D Beyond](https://www.dndbeyond.com/monsters/16803-bearded-devil) | CR 3 | Medium Fiend (Devil) | Lawful Evil

| AC | HP | Speed |
|---|---|---|
| 13 (natural armor) | 52 (8d8+16) | 30 ft. |

**Ability Scores:** STR 16 (+3), DEX 15 (+2), CON 15 (+2), INT 9 (−1), WIS 11 (+0), CHA 11 (+0)  
**Saves:** STR +5, CON +4, WIS +2  
**Resistances:** Cold; B/P/S from nonmagical, non-silvered weapons  
**Immunities:** Fire, Poison | **Condition:** Poisoned  
**Senses:** Darkvision 120 ft.

**Traits:**
- **Devil's Sight.** Magical darkness doesn't impede darkvision.
- **Magic Resistance.** Advantage on saves vs. spells and magical effects.
- **Steadfast.** Can't be frightened while an allied creature is within 30 ft.

- **Beard.** +5 to hit, 5 ft.; Hit 6 (1d8+2) piercing. DC 12 CON or poisoned 1 min *(can't regain HP while poisoned).*

> **Campaign Role:** Warehouse muscle for the cambion smuggling operation. Station 2–3 guarding the shipping manifests. The infernal wound mechanic is punishing at lower levels — consider telegraphing with a bleeding NPC corpse.

**Skills:** Deception +10, Insight +8  
**Senses:** Darkvision 60 ft., Passive 13  

**Damage Vulnerability:** Piercing damage from a magic weapon wielded by a good-aligned creature.  
**Damage Immunity:** B/P/S from nonmagical attacks.  

**Traits:**
- **Limited Magic Immunity.** Cannot be affected or detected by spells of 6th level or lower unless it chooses to be. Advantage on saves against all other spells/magical effects.

**Innate Spellcasting (CHA DC 18, +10 to hit):**
- *At will:* detect thoughts, disguise self, mage hand, minor illusion
- *3/day each:* charm person, detect magic, invisibility, major image, suggestion
- *1/day each:* dominate person, fly, plane shift, true seeing

**Actions (Multiattack — 2 Claw):**
- **Claw.** +7 to hit, 5 ft.; Hit 9 (2d6+2) slashing. The target is cursed — until the curse is removed by *remove curse*, *greater restoration*, or similar, the target cannot regain HP through magical means.

> **Tactics:** Vikramasura opens with *true seeing* (if not already active) and *major image* to create illusory decoys. He prioritises cursing the party healer, then uses *suggestion* to turn the highest-damage party member. If reduced to 30 or fewer HP, he casts *fly* and attempts *plane shift* to the Plane of Shadow. To prevent escape, the party must destroy the anchor gem on Vikramasura's throne before the fight begins (planted during Act 4 recon or given by the Guardian Naga as a reward).
>
> **Critical Mechanic:** His vulnerability to *piercing damage from a magic weapon wielded by a good-aligned creature* is the campaign's central puzzle thread. A neutral paladin, a good-cleric, or a character who has received the *mark of the gods* from the Temple of Indra (see README) qualifies. Without this, the party can still whittle him down — it simply takes far longer.
>
> **Defeating Vikramasura:** He can only be permanently destroyed on the plane he originally contracted with — the Plane of Shadow. Otherwise he reforms after time. The campaign's true ending requires the party to either use the anchor gem to bind him permanently or follow him to his realm for the final blow.
